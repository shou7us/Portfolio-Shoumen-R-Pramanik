CODEX 02 History & Why Past Approaches Failed

How We Got Here

A product that grew into the wrong market segment — and why the exit required more than a feature release.

T1
Origins
Strong SMB Adoption Established
Sogolytics built a loyal base among small teams — product-market fit was real. The platform was celebrated for simplicity, speed, and accessibility. Small organizations valued exactly what enterprise buyers would later dismiss.
T2
Growth Phase
Enterprise Ambition Without Enterprise UX
Marketing began targeting CX Leaders, EX Managers, and Operations Directors at larger organizations. The positioning evolved — but the product workflows did not. A gap opened between what was promised and what was delivered.
T3
The Ceiling
Traditional Paywalls Interrupted Momentum
Paywalls asked for commitment before users felt successful. High-intent users hit feature limits without understanding the value of upgrading. The traditional SaaS model of "trial then pay" collapsed under enterprise-scale evaluation criteria. Users didn't churn from boredom — they churned from insufficient proof.
T4
The LDJ Moment
Lightning Decision Jam Reframes the Problem
Using the Lightning Decision Jam (LDJ) methodology, the team identified the biggest anchor: "High-intent enterprise users hit paywalls before seeing value." The HMW reframe that followed — "How might we use AI to demonstrate enterprise ROI before the user hits a paywall?" — became the strategic foundation for everything that followed.
What We Refused to Repeat
  • Feature explanation as a substitute for feature experience
  • Generic upgrade banners disconnected from the user's felt friction
  • Blank empty states that created "blank canvas anxiety" at onboarding
  • Hard paywalls that interrupted momentum before value was realized
  • Marketing enterprise-grade while delivering SMB-feeling workflows

CODEX 04 Civics, Governance & Compliance

Constraints That Shaped Design

Enterprise buyers in regulated industries don't evaluate governance as a nice-to-have. It's a procurement gate.

🔐
Role-Based Access Control
Healthcare, finance, and education verticals require granular permissions. The Scale tier introduced role-based governance as a core feature — not a bolt-on. Admin, analyst, and viewer roles were scoped to each org's compliance posture.
📋
Auditability & Compliance Visibility
Enterprise users need to demonstrate data stewardship to internal compliance teams. Audit logs, permission trails, and export controls were surfaced as enterprise signals — visible in the Growth Bridge tier as a preview to reduce procurement hesitation.
♿
Accessibility Standards
All filter UI components and onboarding flows were developed within the existing Sogolytics design system with WCAG compliance requirements. K-12 and healthcare verticals in particular require accessible interfaces by institutional policy.
🏛️
Procurement Cycle Alignment
Enterprise procurement cycles average 3–6 months. The AI-orchestrated onboarding was designed specifically to generate "procurement-ready" evidence — executive dashboards and governance previews that internal champions could present to sign-off committees.
🔒
Data Privacy & Sovereignty
Monthly SFTP exports (included in Growth Bridge tier) and usage-based data handling were scoped to ensure enterprise buyers in regulated industries could maintain control over their data and meet internal data residency requirements.
📊
NDA-Safe Visual Design
All case study visuals were produced as illustrative and anonymized representations — compliant with Sogolytics confidentiality requirements. No proprietary UI, branded screens, or client-specific data appears in deliverables shared externally.

Risk If Ignored

Enterprise prospects in healthcare and financial services will exit procurement evaluation the moment governance features cannot be demonstrated. Without visible role-based access, audit logs, and compliance exports — the platform is disqualified before pricing is even discussed. Trust is not earned through marketing; it is demonstrated through the product experience itself.

CODEX 06 Physics & Mechanics

How the Solution Works

From LDJ reframe to Value Bridge Model — a complete walkthrough of the mechanics behind the redesigned enterprise growth experience.

Design Challenge — How Might We?

"How might we use AI to demonstrate enterprise ROI before the user hits a paywall — converting skeptics into champions without asking for commitment first?"

The Value Bridge Model

A tiered PLG framework where progression is earned through usage and outcomes — not blocked by arbitrary limits. This wasn't a pricing exercise. It was a behavioral design system.

Tier 01 — Foundation
Foundation
Design intent: Enterprise credibility from day one — no user should feel like they're using a starter product.
  • One recommended CX/EX template (industry-matched)
  • Limited Advanced Analytics widget using real user data
  • Automation surfaced contextually as "available as your program scales"
  • Enterprise credibility signals embedded in all workflows
"This feels serious and scalable."
Tier 02 — Growth Bridge (New Tier)
Growth Bridge
Design intent: Remove the psychological cliff between free usage and enterprise commitment.
  • 3 custom dashboards (10 widgets each)
  • NPS + basic journey mapping
  • Employee pulse surveys & engagement metrics
  • 500-contact directory with segmentation
  • 2 automation workflows
  • Monthly SFTP exports
"This can actually support our growth."
Tier 03 — Enterprise Scale
Scale
Design intent: Organizational transformation — the product shifts from a tool to an infrastructure layer.
  • Role-based governance for complex org structures
  • Cross-team automation at scale
  • Executive-ready reporting and decision-support
  • Advanced reporting options and custom integrations
  • Full audit logs and compliance visibility
"This is embedded in how we work."

Key Design Tradeoffs
Choices That Paid Off
  • Building within existing design system avoided brand inconsistency and accelerated engineering handoff
  • Multi-agent AI architecture (4 agents vs. 1 general model) produced higher-quality, contextually accurate output
  • LDJ methodology produced a focused, team-aligned design direction in a single session
  • NDA-safe visual language (abstract diagrams, anonymized wireframes) preserved all strategic insight without exposure risk
Deliberate Delays
  • Deep configuration options withheld during onboarding — some power users wanted full control immediately
  • Mobile-responsive governance interface deprioritized in favor of desktop-first enterprise dashboard
  • Natural language filter input scoped to next iteration — too complex to ship within 2-month window
  • ML-based PQL scoring postponed; initial implementation used rule-based threshold triggers

PLG Execution Playbook — 4 Phases

P1
Phase One

Contextual In-Product Value Discovery

Enterprise capabilities appear contextually — embedded within the workflows where they're most relevant. Contextual insights replace generic upgrade banners. Interactive previews use the user's real data, not dummy content.

P2
Phase Two

PQL Detection via Enterprise Behavior Signals

Response volume, survey complexity, export frequency, team invitations, and advanced feature adoption combine into an enterprise readiness score. Above threshold: 48-hour Power User Preview triggered + personalized outreach sent with behavioral context attached.

P3
Phase Three

Expansion via Value-Aligned Pricing

Upgrade paths framed around outcomes, not feature lists. CX and EX expansion pathways positioned by the results they enable. Usage-based pricing components ensure users pay in proportion to realized value — not features unlocked.

P4
Phase Four

Retention Through Progressive, Earned Capability

Long-term users earn permanent enterprise capabilities through sustained engagement — feature graduation, community templates, peer learning. The product grows with the program rather than capping it.

CODEX 09 Psychology & Human Behavior

Enterprise users don't fear complexity. They fear wasted effort. That single insight changed everything about how the product was designed.

"I need to see this will work before I invest more time. Show me the executive dashboard first. Configuration feels risky without knowing the payoff."

— Synthesized from CX Leader interviews, multiple enterprise respondents

"Enterprise users are failing because the product is too complex to learn."

"Enterprise users fear effort that doesn't visibly translate into outcomes. They stop when value feels abstract or delayed — not when features feel hard."

Key Design Iterations Driven by User Feedback

V1 — LDJ
Brainstorm
Identified "blank canvas" as the primary onboarding killer
LDJ surfaced "High-intent enterprise users hit paywalls before seeing value" as the top anchor. HMW reframe redirected the entire design direction from feature explanation to value experience.
V2 — Early Test
Prototype
Growth Bridge tier added after drop-off analysis
Testing revealed a major churn cliff between Foundation and Enterprise tiers. A purpose-built middle tier — Growth Bridge — was added specifically to remove the psychological leap. Without it, 66% of activated users didn't convert.
V3 — AI Pivot
Strategy
Single AI response replaced by 4-agent orchestration
Initial AI onboarding used one generalized model. Quality was inconsistent — generic brand outputs and mismatched templates. Partnering with Engineering to design a multi-agent architecture (Brand, Context, Insight, Visual) produced markedly more credible, org-specific outputs.
V4 — PQL Logic
Systems
PQL scoring refined from binary triggers to weighted readiness score
Initial PQL detection was binary (threshold passed / not passed). User behavior data showed that a combination of signals — 847 responses + 4 surveys + 3 collaborators + 87% feature adoption — was far more predictive than any single trigger. Readiness score model introduced.
CODEX 10 Storytelling & Visual Aesthetics

The Story This Work Tells

Not just what was built — but what it communicates about how this designer thinks.

Cinematic Reference — Slow Thriller / Precision Drama
Confident. Deliberate. High Stakes.
The work feels like a boardroom decision made visible — each design choice carries weight, each system component has a clear role. Nothing is decorative. Everything is purposeful. The aesthetic mirrors the enterprise psychology it serves: trust is built through precision, not decoration.
2-Line Portfolio Manifesto
"I design the moment before the decision — when trust either forms or disappears."
"Enterprise transformation isn't a feature. It's a feeling the product earns."
Rhythm & Hierarchy
Editorial serif for decisions and moments. Mono for system facts. Sans for narrative. Three voices, one visual grammar. Contrast is authority.
Color Philosophy
Deep void background carries weight. Gold is earned — used only for data that matters and actions that move the user forward. Teal marks system intelligence. Red surfaces friction to be resolved.
Space as Trust
Generous negative space signals confidence. Dense information only where data density earns its place (metrics, tables, behavioral systems). Crowded design signals uncertainty. Space signals authority.
Motion Philosophy
Subtle micro-interactions only. Calm transitions between states. Nothing distracts from the data. Animation is reserved for the highest-impact moment: the first reveal of enterprise value.
NDA-Safe Visual Strategy
Abstract diagrams over screenshots. Anonymized wireframes over live UI. Illustrative placeholders that preserve strategic insight without exposure. Confidentiality is the design constraint — not the excuse.
Story Arc
Context → Constraint → Behavior → Aesthetics. The CODEX order mirrors how good designers think. The case study builds the same confidence that the product builds — incrementally, through demonstrated evidence.
