03 · Key Insights

From observation to design implication

Insight 01
Users don't struggle with knowing what they want to ask — they struggle with the effort of expressing it correctly.
User need A starting point that reflects their goal, not a blank canvas.
Implication AI must remove the blank page, not replace the user's judgment.
Insight 02
Logic and branching setup caused the highest abandonment. Users described it as "building a flowchart in my head."
User need Visual, low-cognitive-load branching — closer to a diagram than a settings panel.
Implication AI-assisted logic requires a visual builder, not more fields.
Insight 03
25% of completed surveys contained biased question phrasing — but users had no way to detect this during creation.
User need Real-time quality feedback during creation, not after.
Implication Bias detection must be inline and non-blocking — a nudge, not an interruption.
Insight 04
Branding took 4–6 hours and was disconnected from question creation — users context-switched constantly.
User need Brand identity applied automatically at the start, not retrofitted at the end.
Implication Brand personalisation must happen at registration, not at launch time.

06 · The Solution

Four principles, one coherent system

1

Remove the blank canvas, preserve ownership

Users enter a survey goal in plain language. AI generates a structured starting point — questions, logic, order — which users then edit. They always feel like the author, not the passenger.

2

Brand-first, not brand-last

During registration, users upload a logo or provide a URL. The AI Theme Builder generates brand-aligned colours, fonts, and layout immediately. Branding is done before the first question is written.

3

Contextual quality feedback

Bias detection and readability scoring appear inline as users write — not as a validation gate at the end. Flagged issues show a suggestion, not an error. Users can dismiss or accept.

4

Visual logic, not logic panels

Branching is built as a visual flow diagram. Users drag conditions between questions rather than filling in conditional fields. AI suggests likely branching paths based on question type and prior patterns.

Key Interaction Decisions

📚

Prompt library (< 5 options)

Rather than a free-text prompt field (which created its own blank-canvas anxiety), I designed a curated library of industry- and goal-specific starting points. Testing showed <5 options produced the fastest task completion; more options increased hesitation without improving output quality.

✅

Accept / Modify / Regenerate pattern

Every AI-generated element has three explicit affordances. Users never feel "locked in." This pattern appeared in three different feature areas and became a design system component used across the entire product.

🏷️

Separated AI indicators

AI-generated content is visually distinct from user-edited content throughout the creation flow — a consistent colour and icon treatment that maintains transparency without being intrusive.

09 · What I Didn't Build — And Why

Saying no is a design decision

✕ Full AI Survey Generation (one-click)

Tested well in demos, poorly in real use. Users didn't trust output they didn't contribute to. Ownership mattered more than speed.

✕ Unlimited Prompt Customisation

Free-text prompt fields created their own blank-canvas problem. Curated, focused options produced faster, higher-quality results. Open-ended prompting deferred to Phase 2 power-user setting.

✕ Real-time Collaboration

Analytics showed 91% of survey creation was solo work. Version history with "last edited by" indicators served the 9% who needed collaboration without adding complexity for the majority.

10 · Reflection

What I'd carry into every AI feature I design

1

Perceived control is as important as actual control

Full AI generation was objectively faster but subjectively wrong. Users needed to feel like authors. The goal-first model gave them authorship of the intent, even when AI handled execution. This distinction — ownership of the what, not the how — became a principle I apply to every AI feature I design.

2

Blank canvas anxiety isn't solved by adding more options

The original survey builder had too many starting points, which caused its own paralysis. The curated prompt library worked precisely because we constrained choice. Fewer, better options outperform comprehensive ones when users are under cognitive load.

3

Quality feedback during creation, not after

Bias detection discovered after launch is useless. Placing it inline — as a nudge during writing, not a gate at submission — reduced biased questions without creating friction. The placement decision mattered more than the feature itself.

4

Ethical AI design requires explicit standards, not implicit assumptions

I had to write the rules because none existed. Building the ethical AI guidelines before engineering started — covering transparency, user control, data usage, and bias prevention — shaped implementation decisions that would have been much harder to retrofit.
